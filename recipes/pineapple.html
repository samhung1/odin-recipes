pineapple pizza

Description

Hawaiian pizza is a pizza originating in Canada,[1][2] traditionally topped with pineapple, tomato sauce, cheese, and either ham or bacon.

ingredients

  • pizza dough
  • Pizza sauce
  • Mozzarella cheese, shredded
  • Pineapple chunks (canned or fresh)
  • Ham or Canadian bacon (optional)
  • Olive oil
  • Salt and pepper

Steps

  1. Preheat your oven: Preheat your oven to the temperature specified in your pizza dough recipe or package instructions. Typically, it's around 450°F (230°C).
  2. Prepare the dough: If you're using pre-made pizza dough, follow the package instructions for rolling it out. Alternatively, you can make your own dough from scratch using your preferred recipe.
  3. Spread the sauce: Place the rolled-out dough on a pizza stone or baking sheet lined with parchment paper. Spread a layer of pizza sauce evenly over the dough, leaving a small border around the edges.
  4. Add the cheese: Sprinkle a generous amount of shredded mozzarella cheese over the sauce, covering the entire pizza surface.
  5. Add the pineapple and other toppings: Distribute the pineapple chunks evenly across the pizza. If desired, you can also add ham or Canadian bacon for a classic Hawaiian-style pizza.
  6. Season: Drizzle a little olive oil over the pizza toppings, and sprinkle with salt and pepper to taste. This step is optional but can enhance the flavors.
  7. Bake the pizza: Place the pizza in the preheated oven and bake according to the dough recipe or package instructions, usually around 12-15 minutes. Keep an eye on it to prevent burning.
  8. Slice and serve: Once the cheese is melted and bubbly, and the crust is golden brown, remove the pizza from the oven. Let it cool for a few minutes, then slice it into wedges or squares. Serve hot and enjoy!
